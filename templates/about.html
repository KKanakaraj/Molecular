About

The World Health Organization predicts that 15% of the developed countries population will be affected by major depressive disorders, hence our social interactions are expected to change in the next decade. Our project aims at highlighting how hormones could influence people's day to day life, since they have an important impact on social behavior. Thus, we discovered that there are several hormones involved in social behavior. We discussed the main factors related to oxytocin, dopamine, estrogen, testosterone and vasopressin. All of them are highly involved in social and psychological disorders such as aggressiveness, anxiety, depression and schizophrenia. For each of these hormones we carried out a brief summary of their biological mechanism followed by a detailed explanation of their role in these types of disorders. The main hormone we focused on is oxytocin, as it has been shown to have a strong influence on social interactions. We figured out that low oxytocin levels lead to a deficit in social behavior, in particular depression and anxiety, and it is mainly due to genetic or epigenetic deregulations. Instead, low dopamine levels cause slow psychological activity and the main causes of dopamine disorders are linked to abuse of drugs. Moreover, estrogen and testosterone have a severe impact on aggressiveness and sexual behaviour. Finally, estrogen and vasopressin have also an effect on memory and learning and vasopressin represents also a possible biomarker for treating autism spectrum disorders (ASD).
